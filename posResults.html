Hip operation

Personal

Average recovery time

1-6 months

Possible long-term complications

  • Risk of blood clots
  • Risk of infection
  • Fracturing around the artificial hip
  • Risk of dislocation
  • Change in leg length
  • Risk of joint loosening

Care after your operation

You will likely need physiotherapy to regain full mobility. You will likely need to pay for this yourself.

CCS mobility permit

You may be eligibe for a CCS mobility permit. A CCS mobility permit allows you to park in specially marked mobility parks, which are wider and closer to entrances.

Financial

Income

Help with costs related to operation

Disability allowance

You may be eligible to receive the Disability Allowance. The disability allowance can help with costs such as medication , transportation to doctors appointments and more.

Help with the costs of home/vehicle modification

House modification funding

You may be able to receive help with the costs of modifications necessary for your disability. Your eligibility for the funding is decided by the Ministry of Health

Vehicle modification funding

You may be able to recieve help with the cost of modifying your vehicle to accomodate for your disability. In some cases you may be able to get help with the purchase of a vehicle. You'll need to talk to your occupational therapist, who will complete an assessment of your needs.
